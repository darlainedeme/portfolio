About Me

I am Edeme Darlain, an Energy Policy Analyst specializing in renewable energy systems, geospatial data analysis, and sustainable energy solutions. With a strong background in electrical engineering and experience working with international organizations, I have contributed to numerous projects aimed at improving energy access and efficiency across Africa. I hold a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering and am a certified engineer with a passion for integrating advanced technologies into energy systems.

In my professional career, I have served as an Africa Energy Policy Analyst at the International Energy Agency, focusing on geospatial data modelling and providing policy advice for planning national and regional energy systems in Africa. My role involved extensive research and analysis, contributing to reports such as the IEA World Energy Outlook 2022 and the IEA Africa Energy Outlook 2022. Prior to this, I was a Research Fellow at the Polytechnic University of Milan, where I conducted research on energy planning and sustainability, developing models for integrating renewable energy sources into national grids.

My academic background includes a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Milan, where I specialized in renewable energy integration and smart grid technologies. I also hold an M.Sc. in ICT for Development from the University of Turin, focusing on sustainable development through information and communication technology applications. Additionally, I have experience in philosophical studies from the University of Milan, and degrees in Energy Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Milan.
